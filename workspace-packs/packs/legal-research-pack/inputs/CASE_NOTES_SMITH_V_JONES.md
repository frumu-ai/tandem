# Case Notes: Smith v. Jones (2024)

**Court:** Superior Court of the State of Columbia
**Case No:** CV-2024-00123
**Plaintiff:** Arthur Smith (Former Employee)
**Defendant:** Jones Tech Innovations, Inc. (Employer)
**Topic:** Intellectual Property Rights & Wrongful Termination

## Facts of the Case
- **Plaintiff** was employed as a Senior Engineer at Jones Tech from 2020-2023.
- **Plaintiff** developed a "compression algorithm" on his personal laptop during weekends.
- **Defendant** claims ownership of the algorithm based on the "Inventions Assignment" clause in the employment contract.
- **Plaintiff** was fired after refusing to hand over the source code.
- **Plaintiff** sues for wrongful termination and declaratory judgment of ownership.

## Key Arguments

### Plaintiff's Argument
- Work was done entirely on own time, own equipment, and without using company resources (Labor Code ยง 2870).
- The algorithm is unrelated to Defendant's current business (social media app), as it is a general file compression tool.

### Defendant's Argument
- Employment contract explicitly states "all inventions conceived during the term of employment relate to the Company's actual or *demonstrably anticipated* research."
- Company was planning to branch into cloud storage (which requires compression), thus the invention belongs to them.
- Plaintiff used experience gained at the company to build the tool.

## Procedural History
- Motion to Dismiss by Defendant denied.
- Discovery phase currently ongoing.
- Depositions of CTO scheduled for next week.

## Judge's Interim Remarks
> "The crux of the matter will likely turn on the interpretation of 'demonstrably anticipated' business. The burden is on the employer to prove this was in the pipeline before the employee's invention."
